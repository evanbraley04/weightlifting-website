Overview

Supplements can be a great way to enhance your gym experience and also speed up progress in the gym. For me personally, I have experimented with three supplements...protein powder, pre-workout, and creatine. I have varying opinions on each, so, on this page, I will go through my thoughts on each of the three supplements that I have tried.

Protein Powder

Protein powder is a supplement that gives you a big dose of protein in a quick-to-consume fashion. I have tried numerous different protein powders and my opinion on all of them is the same. Protein powder can be a great thing, especially when you are trying to hit a certain protein goal per day, but overall they aren't necessary. Protein powder is just that, protein, and you can meet all of your protein goals easily if you are paying close attention to what you eat and making smart decisions. Protein powder isn't bad, as it can be a little boost to hitting your goals, but all of the people who say you need to use it are wrong. So, my opinion on protein powder is essentially, use it if you want, if not it doesn't really matter. Personally, I will use a protein shake whenever I feel I need one, but it isn't something that I use heavily.

Pre-Workout

Pre-workout is something that I have a little less experience with. I've tried dedicated pre-workout powders and energy drinks, but I've never really felt the need to use pre-workout except in rare situations where I'm just doing it for fun. Pre-workout is essentially just a cocktail of various ingredients, the main one usually being caffeine, and although it can give you a little extra energy, it really isn't something that you need, and isn't even something that I would recommend, as drinking copious amounts of caffeine all the time is pretty bad for your health. So, in my opinion, pre-workout isn't something I would personally recommend.

Creatine

Creatine is the supplement that I probably have the most experience with. I used creatine every single day over a period of about 7 months. A creatine supplement is essentially either a powder or pill that contains creatine, which is something that you typically intake from things like red meat and seafood. When you take creatine as a supplement, the cells in your muscles essentially produce more energy during lifting. While on creatine I did notice I was able to lift more weight and do so for longer. Additionally, my muscles looked a lot fuller, but this is in part due to creatine causing your muscle cells to hold more water. Overall, I really did see great benefits to using creatine, but I stopped for a few reasons. One is that when on creatine, your body gets dehydrated a lot easier, so you have to drink a ton of water, and for me personally, I strangely noticed that using creatine caused me to have a lot more acne than usual, and there is anecdotal evidence to suggest that creatine could exacerbate acne. So, due to these reasons as well as the cost of having to constantly purchase creatine, I stopped using it. My recommendation is to try creatine out and see if you like the results you are getting from it, and then make a decision based on that.
